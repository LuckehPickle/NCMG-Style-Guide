Standard Colours

A deep dive into the colour palette of North Coast Marble & Granite, its history, and its future on the web.

Skip discussion.

Why bother?

Colour is the most important tool in a designer's belt. It establishes a brand's identity, and the emotions we feel when we engage with it. So it's vital that we pick a palette that feels right.

We're aiming for a modern, professional look. Something reliable, trustworthy, but not without a hint of rigour. So it's time to look back on the history of NCMG's colour palette, to decide what stays, and what goes.

Embracing history

Just in case you've forgotten, here's what the original site looked like. Errors messages and all.

The result is a colour palette that (in my opinion) feels washed out or bland. As a result, we as viewers feel the same emotions towards NCMG. Obviously, that needs to change, but we'll use the existing colour palette as a jumping off point.

A fresh coat of paint

After reworking the above palette, here's what I came up with. Each colour has a corresponding Sass variable (hence the dollar sign). There is also an inverted variant of each colour grouping, accessible with the -inverted suffix. The inverted colour can be used for text or elements on top of it's corresponding colour.
